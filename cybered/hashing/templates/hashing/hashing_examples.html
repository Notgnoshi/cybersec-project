Hashing

Example Hashes

One of the most widely known hash algorithms is the MD5 sum; or Message Digest 5. It was developed in 1992 as a successor the MD4 algorithm. Although it is now considered unsecure, it is still used in a lot of applications because it is 'good enough' or because switching would require a large effort.

Another well-known series of hash algorithms are the SHA series; SHA0, SHA1, SHA2, and SHA3 have all been developed as increasingly secure standards of hashing. As computers become faster, algorithms like the SHA series become less secure; at this point, SHA0 and SHA1 are no longer acceptable, and SHA2 is close to becoming obselete.
