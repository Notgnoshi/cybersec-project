Introduction

Steganography (/ˌstɛɡəˈnɒɡrəfi/ STEG-ə-NOG-rə-fee) is the practice of concealing a file, message, image, or video within another file, message, image, or video. The word steganography combines the Greek words steganos (στεγανός), meaning "covered, concealed, or protected", and graphein (γράφειν) meaning "writing".

--Wikipedia

As the definition states, steganography is, in its simplest form, the art and science of hiding data inside of other data. See if you can find the hidden message in the following text:

this is a very long sentence with a lot of words in it. You might find that it is confusing, because it just talks circles, but you are being bamboozled! if you Look carefully, you'll notice that there's a lot of Nice details in the message, and even some hidden information. Today you are learning about basic steganography, but who knows what surprises tomorrow will bring.

If you take only the words with capital letters, you can see the hidden message: You Look Nice Today.

This is a very simplistic example of steganography, you may have seen it before in the plot of a book or movie, or maybe you've even used it yourself!
